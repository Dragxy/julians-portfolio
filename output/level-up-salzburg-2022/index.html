LEVEL UP, Salzburg, 2022

The second edition of LEVEL UP – The Gaming Festival took place on June 29 and 30 at the Salzburg Exhibition Center, drawing around 10,000 enthusiastic visitors. With a vibrant mix of eSports tournaments, interactive experiences, fascinating exhibitors, and impressive cosplay performances, the event offered something for everyone. After this successful weekend, we’re already looking forward to the next edition on June 28 and 29, 2023!

Mainstage: Spotlight on eSports and Diversity

The mainstage of LEVEL UP 2022 was the hotspot for eSports fans and professionals alike. High-caliber tournaments captivated the audience, featuring thrilling competitions in games like Rocket League, Counter-Strike 2, and Super Smash Bros. The electrifying atmosphere made the mainstage the festival’s central highlight.

A standout feature on the mainstage was the SheRivals: Queens of Austria show match. This unique League of Legends tournament featured female teams from NNO Prime and SK Gaming, marking a bold statement for greater diversity and visibility for women in eSports.

Lena Braito, project manager of LEVEL UP, emphasized the importance of inclusion:
“At LEVEL UP, we place great value on diversity. That’s why, for the first time, we organized an all-female tournament this year in collaboration with our long-standing partners, Morekats Gaming. Our goal is to inspire young women and girls and challenge traditional gender roles.”

Excitement and Fun on the Raiffeisen Club Community Stage

The Raiffeisen Club Community Stage featured a range of interactive programs where visitors could actively participate. Highlights included the LudeCat, where participants competed in indie games in four-player matches. At Game2Help – Beat the Pro, daring challengers faced off against Super Smash pro and YouTuber Luigikid, showcasing their skills. For every exceptional performance against the pro, €200 was added to the donation pool for Letsplay4Charity. Visitors also showcased their gaming and pop culture knowledge during The Amazing Nerdquiz – LEVEL UP Edition.

The stage even allowed attendees to get a taste of the spotlight themselves!

Streaming for a Good Cause

The Letsplay4Charity livestream was another massive success. Popular streamers gathered live from the festival to raise money for charitable causes through their Twitch streams. Over 281,000 viewers tuned in and donated directly during the broadcast.

In addition, the Beat the Pro Challenge from Game2Help contributed to the charity drive, helping raise a remarkable total of €11,000 for various causes.

Indie Games, Retro Gaming, and More in the Gaming Zone

The Gaming Zone was an exciting hub featuring exhibitors and teams showcasing innovative offerings. Highlights included an escape room by Salzburg AG, a digger simulator by Porr, and sim racing at the Red Bull Gaming Ground. Companies like BRZ, W&H, and Axess also used the event to recruit talent.

The Indie Games Area, presented by FH Salzburg, attracted visitors interested in the creativity and innovation of independent developers. Thirty game creators showcased their projects, offering attendees a glimpse into the world of unique, off-the-beaten-path games.

Adjacent to the Gaming Zone, the Retro Area was a paradise for fans of classic games, featuring iconic titles, pinball machines, and vintage gaming consoles.

From Art to Action: Beyond Screens

The Artist Alley was the go-to place for fans of creative craftsmanship. Featuring hand-made artwork, posters, jewelry, and fan merchandise, this lively zone celebrated the artistic side of gaming culture. Next door, the Tabletop Area offered a cozy spot for strategy, board, and card game enthusiasts to try out new games or join tournaments.

For those seeking action, the Action Area delivered thrilling experiences. Visitors tested their skills on KTM minibikes, engaged in tactical matches of laser tag, or had fun in Bubble Soccer—bouncing around in giant inflatable balls. VR matches blurred the lines between virtual and real, adding an extra layer of excitement.

Looking Ahead to LEVEL UP 2023

Alexander Kribus, Managing Director of the Salzburg Exhibition Center, expressed his delight:
“LEVEL UP exceeded all expectations this year with its diversity and interactive programming. The positive feedback and enthusiasm of the visitors made the event a phenomenal success. We are already looking forward to continuing this momentum next year.”

Mark your calendars! LEVEL UP 2023 is happening on June 28 and 29, and we can’t wait to see you there!
