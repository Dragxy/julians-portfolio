Marine Biology Week 19/20

School Year 19/20 – MINT Classes

The 4th-grade students of the science-focused branch spent a week in Croatia.

On July 2, 2019, the two science classes, 4AR and 4BR, departed from St. Johann heading to Croatia. On Monday, students picked up their snorkeling gear and tried on their wetsuits for the first time to ensure a proper fit.

Day 1: Getting Started

Well-equipped, the two classes began their first project day separately on Tuesday.

  • 4AR:
    The class boarded a boat that took them to the uninhabited island of Plavnik after an hour’s journey. Upon arrival, they received an introduction to snorkeling before diving into the sea to explore marine life. Among the discoveries were starfish, sea cucumbers, and a variety of fish. In the afternoon, the group moved to a plateau, where they caught the native Apulian tarantula and found other creatures such as a praying mantis.

  • 4BR:
    This class spent their first day snorkeling in a bay. In the afternoon, they participated in a scavenger hunt, where they discovered, caught, and in some cases, even tasted various organisms. Each group presented their findings in detail to the rest of the class through small presentations.

Day 2: Switching Programs

On Wednesday, the two classes swapped activities. The only difference was that the 4AR also had the opportunity to explore an abandoned hotel.

Day 3: A Shared Adventure

On Thursday, both classes traveled together to another bay for their final snorkeling session. In the afternoon, they focused on various activities, such as collecting waste to contribute to environmental conservation.

Evening and Departure

That evening, everyone went on a group trip to the town of Krk. On Friday, after breakfast, the students headed back to Austria.
